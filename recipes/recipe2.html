KOREANFRIED CHICKEN
Yield:4 Servings
Prep Time:10 minutes Cooking Time:15 minutes Total Time:25 minutes
Description The main process that makes the Korean Fried Chicken recipe healthy is creating the mixture sauce from scratch. For the chicken marinade, using almond milk for tenderizing the meat comes with other benefits, like being low in calories, dairy-free, doesn’t raise blood sugar and reducing the risk of heart disease. Using maple syrup for the sauce enhances the flavor, and the benefit of the syrup is high in antioxidants.
Notes & Tips Creating your Korean spicy sauce is better than store-bought since most store-bought Korean spicy sauce contains lots of refined sugar that are unhealthy.
Ingredients:
  • 3 tbsp gochujang paste
  • 2 tbsp rice wine vinegar
  • 2 tsp sesame oil
  • 1 ½ tbsp maple syrup
  • 450g boneless chicken thigh, cut into bite-sized pieces
  • ½ tsp salt
  • ¼ tsp ground black pepper
  • 6 cloves garlic, minced
  • ½ cup almond milk
  • 1 1/2 cups cornstarch
  • 1 cup all-purpose flour
  • 1 tbsp garlic powder
  • 1 tbsp onion powder
  • 1 tsp paprika powder
  • 400ml vegetable oil
Directions:
  1. Combine gochujang paste, rice wine vinegar, sesame oil, and maple syrup in a bowl. Set aside.
  2. Whisk together paprika powder, garlic powder, onion powder, flour, and cornstarch; set aside
  3. Season the boneless chicken thigh with salt, ground black pepper, and minced garlic.
  4. Add the almond milk and mix well.
  5. Sprinkle half of the flour mixture and mix well.
  6. Dredge the coated chicken into the remaining flour mixture.
  7. Fry the chicken until golden brown and place in a large bowl.
  8. Drizzle with the gochujang paste and mix well.
  9. Serve immediately.

Made Healthier
The Korean fried chicken base recipe uses instant Korean chicken sauce. This instant product contains artificial substances like corn syrup and fermented ethanol that may harm a person’s health when regularly consumed. The experts recommend making the glaze from scratch.
